CHANDRAGUPTA MAURYA

The Founder of the Mauryan Empire (322–298 BCE)

FOR UPSC | KPSC | SSC | RAILWAYS

1. Origins & The Rise to Power

ಉಗಮ ಮತ್ತು ಅಧಿಕಾರಕ್ಕೆ ಏರಿಕೆ
  • Founder: Established the Maurya Dynasty after overthrowing Dhana Nanda (the last Nanda ruler).
  • The Mentor: Guided by Chanakya (Kautilya/Vishnugupta), the author of Arthashastra.
  • Greek Records: Greek historians called him Sandrocottus or Androcottus.
  • Lineage: Buddhist texts link him to the Moriya Kshatriya clan, while Puranas suggest a humble origin.
💡 Exam Tip: Mudrarakshasa (by Vishakhadatta) is the Sanskrit play that details how Chanakya helped Chandragupta win.

2. Expansion & Seleucus Conflict

ದಿಗ್ವಿಜಯಗಳು ಮತ್ತು ಸೆಲ್ಯೂಕಸ್‌ನೊಂದಿಗೆ ಸಂಘರ್ಷ
  • Defeat of Seleucus (305 BCE): Defeated Seleucus I Nicator, the Greek successor of Alexander.
  • The Treaty: Chandragupta gained Aria (Herat), Arachosia (Kandahar), and Gedrosia (Baluchistan).
  • Marriage Alliance: Seleucus married his daughter (Helena) to Chandragupta.
  • The Gift: In exchange, Chandragupta gifted 500 war elephants to the Greeks.
⭐ Remember: This made him the first Indian king to rule territory beyond the Hindu Kush mountains!

  • Army: Maintained a huge permanent army (Infantry, Cavalry, Chariots, Elephants).

4. Religion & The End of Reign

ಧರ್ಮ ಮತ್ತು ಅಂತಿಮ ದಿನಗಳು
  • Jainism: In his later years, he embraced Jainism under the monk Bhadrabahu.
  • Abdication: He gave up the throne to his son, Bindusara.
  • The Karnataka Connection: Travelled to Shravanabelagola (Hassan District).
  • Death: Died by Sallekhana (fasting to death) in approx 298 BCE at Chandragiri Hill.
📍 Local History Tip: KPSC frequently asks about the "Chandragiri" hill in Shravanabelagola named after him.
